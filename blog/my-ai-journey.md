# From Teaching to Building: My Journey in AI

**Published**: May 2026 | **Read time**: 7 min

Three years ago, I started training organizations on AI. Today, I'm transitioning to building production AI systems. This is the story of that journey and why both are essential.

---

## The Teaching Years (2023-2026)

### What I Did

For three years, I trained teams across:
- 🚚 Logistics companies
- 💼 SMEs and startups
- 📢 Marketing and sales organizations
- 🎓 Educational institutions
- 🏭 Manufacturing and industrial sectors

### What I Learned

It wasn't just about teaching ML algorithms. The real lessons were:

**Lesson 1: Understanding > Techniques**
Organizations don't need to know every algorithm. They need to understand:
- What AI can and cannot do
- When to apply it
- How to measure success
- What risks to manage

**Lesson 2: Implementation Is the Real Challenge**
Building a model is easy. Integrating it into operations is hard.
- Data quality issues are everywhere
- Business processes are more complex than anticipated
- Change management is critical
- ROI needs to be measurable

**Lesson 3: Context Matters Enormously**
The same AI technique works differently in:
- Different industries
- Different company sizes
- Different organizational maturity levels
- Different regulatory environments

**Lesson 4: People > Technology**
The organizations that succeeded had:
- Champions within the organization
- Willingness to experiment and learn
- Patience with iteration
- Focus on business outcomes, not technical perfection

The ones that struggled often had:
- Unrealistic expectations from the start
- Siloed teams (data science separate from operations)
- Focus on "AI for AI's sake"
- Lack of executive support

## Why I'm Transitioning to Building

### The Teaching Limits

After three years, I realized training alone has limits:
- **Accountability**: I teach and leave; others execute
- **Risk**: My mistakes are someone else's problem
- **Scale**: One trainer reaches limited people
- **Impact**: Indirect impact on outcomes
- **Learning**: Teaching prevents hands-on problem-solving

### The Building Opportunity

Building production systems offers:
- **Accountability**: I own the outcomes
- **Learning**: Real problems require real innovation
- **Scale**: One system serves thousands
- **Impact**: Direct measurement of value created
- **Mastery**: Deep expertise through doing

### What Training Taught Me for Building

Those teaching years were the best preparation for building:

1. **Diverse Industry Knowledge** — I understand multiple sectors deeply
2. **Common Patterns** — I've seen what works and what fails across contexts
3. **Communication Skills** — I can explain complex systems clearly
4. **User Empathy** — I understand what teams actually need
5. **Implementation Reality** — I know the gap between theory and practice
